What is quidditch?

Quidditch is a fast-paced, mixed-gender, full contact sport that is rapidly growing in popularity across the world. Invented in the USA in 2005, the sport has since spread across the world and is now played in more than 40 countries.

Quidditch players compete to score goals by throwing a quaffle (deflated volleyball) through the opposing team’s hoops while avoiding being hit by bludgers (dodgeballs) thrown by the opposing team’s beaters. The game ends when the snitch, a tennis ball attached to an impartial official, is caught by one team’s seeker.

About the Club

The Holyrood Hippogriffs are one of the oldest quidditch clubs in the UK. Founded in 2011, we are a community club based in Edinburgh and consisting of two teams, who compete at high level in the UK national league:

  • Holyrood Hippogriff Firsts have consistently come out as one of the top 8 teams in the UK over the last three seasons.
  • Holyrood Hippogriff Seconds prioritise development of individual players over winning medals, but are still a competitive team and have qualified for the last two British Quidditch cups, the national championship.

Highlander Tournament

Every October, Holyrood Hippogriffs hosts Highlander, the longest running quidditch tournament in the UK. 12th-13th October 2019 was its 7th iteration, hosted in Inverleith Park, with participating teams from Scotland, the rest of the UK, and Europe.
